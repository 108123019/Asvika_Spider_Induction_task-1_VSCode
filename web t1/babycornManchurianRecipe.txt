Prepare Baby Corn:

Rinse the baby corn pieces and pat them dry with a kitchen towel.
In a bowl, mix corn flour, all-purpose flour, red chili powder, and salt.
Add the baby corn pieces to the flour mixture and toss well to coat evenly.
Fry Baby Corn:

Heat oil for deep frying in a pan or kadai over medium-high heat.
Carefully drop the coated baby corn pieces into the hot oil.
Fry in batches until they turn golden brown and crispy.
Remove from oil using a slotted spoon and drain excess oil on paper towels. Set aside.
Make the Manchurian Sauce:

Heat 1 tablespoon of oil in a wok or large skillet over medium-high heat.
Add finely chopped garlic, ginger, and green chilies. Saut√© for a minute until aromatic.
Add Vegetables:

Add finely chopped onion and thinly sliced bell pepper (capsicum). Stir-fry for 2-3 minutes until the vegetables are slightly tender yet crisp.
Prepare the Sauce:

Add tomato ketchup, soy sauce, chili sauce, vinegar or lemon juice, sugar, and salt to taste. Mix well.
Thicken the Sauce:

Pour in 1 cup of water and bring the mixture to a simmer.
In a small bowl, mix 1 tablespoon of corn flour with 3 tablespoons of water to make a slurry. Gradually add the corn flour slurry to the simmering sauce, stirring continuously until the sauce thickens to a gravy consistency.
Add Fried Baby Corn:

Once the sauce thickens, add the fried baby corn pieces to the wok or skillet. Toss well until the baby corn pieces are well coated with the Manchurian sauce.
Finish and Serve:

Garnish with chopped spring onions.
Serve hot baby corn Manchurian as a delicious appetizer or as a side dish with fried rice or noodles.
